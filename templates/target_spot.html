Tomato Target Spot

Target spot of tomato is caused by the fungal pathogen Corynespora cassiicola. 1 The disease occurs on field-grown tomatoes in tropical and subtropical regions of the world. Target spot was first observed on tomatoes in the U.S. in Immokalee, Florida in 1967.

Symptoms

Target spot symptoms begin as small dark lesions which enlarges to form light brown lesions with concentric pattern and a yellow halo around it in the transplants.

Causes

Target spot of tomato is caused by the fungal pathogen Corynespora cassiicola.

Control and Treatment

The primary strategy used to manage target spot on tomato is the regular application of fungicides. Applications should begin before symptoms appear when conditions are favorable for infection and disease development.
